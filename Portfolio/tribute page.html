Hachikō

Symbol of loyalty

Timeline

  • Born on November 10, 1923 in Ōdate, Japan.
  • In 1924 Hidesaburō Ueno, a professor at the Tokyo Imperial University took Hachikō as a pet an brought him to live in Shibuya, Tokyo.
  • From that point forwards, Ueno would go to work and Hachikō would greet him at the end of the day at the station.
  • In May 21, 1925 Ueno did not return. He died during class and never returned to the train station.
  • For almost 10 years, Hachikō awaited his return appearing exactly when the train was due at the station.
  • One of Ueno students learned the story of Hachikō's life, visited him frequently and published several articles about the dog's impressive loyalty.
  • Eventually, Hachikō became a nation-wide symbol of loyalty.
  • On March 8, 1935 Hachikō died. His remains were cremated and his ashes buried next to Professor Ueno's.

“Never mind, said Hachiko each day. Here I wait, for my friend who’s late. I will stay, just to walk beside you for one more day.”
